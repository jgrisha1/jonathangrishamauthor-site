FILE // 001

The Devil Finds a Home

Stand-alone · Extreme Horror

A family relocates to a farmhouse on the rural edges of nowhere. The neighbours are friendly. The soil is rich with something that was once alive and refuses to stop feeding. By the third week, the youngest child begins leaving gifts at the treeline — gifts that belong to people who disappeared before the family arrived. A novel about inheritance, appetite, and the domesticity of evil.

The Choir Beneath the Floor

Stand-alone · Extreme Horror

After a mid-century apartment building is condemned, a structural engineer discovers that the walls are laced with hair — human hair, woven into the load-bearing columns as if by design, still warm to the touch. The building's original blueprints list forty-seven rooms. The city permits show thirty-nine. Nobody has ever asked where the other eight went.

FILE // 003

Pale Meridian

Series: The Threshold // Book 1

A forensic linguist is hired to transcribe recordings made inside a decade-long cult compound. The audio is clear. The cult members all speak in the same voice, regardless of age or sex — a voice that was documented on the first day of the investigation and has not changed since, even though that investigator died in 1997. Language as contagion. Meaning as a vector for something that does not have a clinical name yet but leaves the same lesion pattern on every brain that encounters it.

FILE // 004

The Cartography of Opened Things

Stand-alone · Body Horror / Extreme

A surgeon who has performed thousands of autopsies begins finding evidence inside her patients of surgeries that have no corresponding medical records, scars from instruments that do not exist, and cavities where organs should be — cavities lined with something she recognises from her childhood bedroom wallpaper. A careful, precise exploration of bodily autonomy and the horror of being mapped from the inside without consent.

FILE // 005

After the Threshold

Series: The Threshold // Book 2

Picking up eighteen months after Pale Meridian, the only surviving investigator attempts to rebuild a normal life. The lesion on her MRI has not grown. It has not shrunk either. It has learned to mimic the surrounding tissue so precisely that her neurologist is no longer certain it was ever a lesion at all. A sequel about the violence of recovery and the things we house in ourselves long after we have been declared cured.

A Lullaby for the End of Appetite

Stand-alone · Extreme Horror

A grief counsellor specialising in childhood bereavement notices that several of her clients share an identical recurring dream — same room, same figure, same instruction delivered in the same sequence of non-language sounds that her clients can reproduce perfectly even though they have never met each other, sounds that a phonetician identifies as belonging to no documented human language and no documented animal. The figure wears the face of whatever the child loved most.

When Obedience

Jonathan Grisham · Non-Fiction · Cult / Coercive Control

An examination of the mechanism of obedience inside coercive religious and institutional systems — how it is demanded, how it is enforced, and what it costs the people required to perform it. Grisham writes from the inside of the system he is describing. Essential reading for anyone trying to understand how control operates at the level of the individual conscience.
